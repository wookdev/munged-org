Something that has been on this site, in this section, but never linked to before: The one and only Enterprise Services Offsite meeting. Blackmail pictures included. I guess the time is right for this to be exposed to the outside world.
The 1999 East Coast RiverSoft Holiday Party was a blast. We rented out Liquids, and invited Softees, customers, and partners. These are the pictures of that night.

The Networld+InterOp trade show is one of the premier general IT trade shows in the US. It happens twice a year. In the spring in Las Vegas, and in the fall in Atlanta. This was N+I Atlanta 1999, my first trade show as an "Exhibitor".
Enterprise Services did a Beta Project kick-off meeting with the beta customers in Leicestershire (sp?). At left, is the only picture of this I took. We were going to have a wrap-up meeting at the Ice Hotel the following Feb., and the ice carvings where a demonstration of what we would get. Finally, we have Jon and ET drawing each other's countries. Notice how the UK looks like a moose.
Assorted pictures that I just have kicking around. I didn't take all of them, but what the hell, I have them in the library. Unlike most of the others, most of these are not actually taken at official RiverSoft events, but include Softees. Descriptions included within...

The following galleries, are all RiverSoft pictures I didn't take. Most of these were up on The Brain (RiverSoft's intranet site) for a while. They were eventually taken down because they weren't current.

My thanks to Mitch Read, the riv_webmaster, for forwarding these to me, and to Ken Vanderweel for connecting me with Mitch.

Mike Southon took Polaroids of everyone that joined RiverSoft, way back in the early days. These are the pictures that are left. The first 41 employees are captured on "film", in bad light, and completely unprepared to be immortalized forever. I, personally, wouldn't have had it any other way.
I suspect that these pictures are a Holiday party in San Francisco. I know these are SF softees. These pictures where on the brain for a while. I didn't take these, so I can't be sure.
RiverSoft held two developer's meeting in Chamonix. A place isolated in the mountains of France. Given these pictures, I have no idea how any of these people got any work done. I would have been enraptured by the view.
RiverSoft was 5 years old, February 14th, 2002, having been formed 5 years earlier on St. Valentine's day. A birthday bash was held in the Software Factory.
The 2001 holiday party in London. A black tie affair. I missed this one by a day. I forget exactly why, or it was even for a good reason. But A Good Time was had by all.
