Wired Magazine

We happened to pick up a copy of Wired Magazine recently in a airport. It was interesting, including the details of how they made the previous cover change colors when it was touched.

The part that was interesting was actually more to do with what wasn't in the magazine. Despite claiming to be a magazine for the "wired generation" or whatnot (that is my quote, not theirs since I'm to lazy to actually look this up), it looked more like Playboy, but without the T&A. The major difference between the two as far as I can tell, is that it is actually possible to differentiate the ads from the content in Playboy.

If there is a defense for Wired, it could possibly be that the net has so invaded pop culture, or maybe vise-versa, that it isn't possible for Wired to look any different than it does. Sex sells, as does over-the-top graphics, it would seem. The techno culture likes a perverted kind of cool, and flash can often fool the masses.

Oh well.
